# About Me
**I’m a designer who lives in the 'messy middle' of complex systems, but outside of work, I’m usually found with a guitar in my hand, hunting for the best food in the city, or drawing weird things that have nothing to do with spreadsheets.**

I turn fragile, manual workflows into robust systems. My journey into design didn't start in a studio—it started by watching people struggle with broken software, and deciding I needed to be the one to fix it.

## The Journey Here

### The Deliveroo "Aha" Moment
My transition into UX started before I even knew the title existed. While working at Deliveroo, I was put on a project helping local restaurants get up and running. Hearing how much they struggled with bad processes and broken software to earn their revenue hit hard. I started doing product and UX work simply to fix their problems—and realized I had found my career.

### The Control Room & The Master's
Determined to make the pivot official, I moved to New Zealand. While completing my Master’s of UX Design, I worked in the control room at Meridian Energy. As the "voice on the radio" for field crews, I managed safety protocols where systemic reliability was the baseline for survival. It taught me how to handle high-stakes operational chaos.

### The PikPok Chapter
I took that logistical mindset into the complex ecosystem of F2P mobile games at PikPok. Within six months, I was promoted to Senior UX Designer. I spent my time navigating the shifting sands of pre-launch titles, establishing localization pipelines for 18+ languages, and pushing back to ensure our game systems prioritized the player's experience over dark patterns.

### Advocate for the Human
My Master’s capstone proved that a medical tracker's biggest flaw wasn't the device itself, but the lack of communication between the people using it. Whether I'm designing hardware service blueprints or pushing back against technical teams in F2P gaming to protect a player from a negative experience, I make sure the human stays at the center of the system.

---

{ type="zig-zag-grid", imagePos="right" }

### Always Be Mapping
I am an information sponge. I don't wait for a brief; I look for gaps where data gets lost or people get frustrated. That’s how I discovered a massive architectural flaw in a game's core navigation—I just started mapping the UI to see how it worked. I put my hand up, ask questions, and pull threads until the picture is clear.

### One Size Never Fits All
I don't believe in forcing rigid UX frameworks onto messy realities. When I first joined PikPok, standard agile processes were fragmented and UX buy-in was low. Instead of fighting the current, I adapted my approach. I stepped up to lead sprint reviews, facilitated cross-functional ideation sessions, and found organic ways to bring UX into the conversation earlier. I change my toolkit to fit the problem—not the other way around.

## When I'm Not in Figma...
I’m a designer who lives in the "messy middle" of complex systems, but off-screen, things are a bit more eclectic. 

I used to have the fringe and play guitar in a heavy metal band. These days, I've traded the distortion pedal for an acoustic guitar, playing folk and indie music (usually with my cats in the room).

I am always on the hunt for the city's best burger, but ever since a recent trip to Japan, my obsession has pivoted entirely to finding the perfect bowl of Tonkotsu Ramen. 

I also love creating geometric and abstract art. Currently, my favorite piece is a cartoon painting I did of an alligator in red boots smoking a cigarette. Next up: trying to master watercolors.

And when I just want to switch my brain off, I have a massive soft spot for old-school cinema. You can usually find me rewatching *Die Hard*, the early *Fast & Furious* movies, *Gone in 60 Seconds*, or diving into classic sci-fi.
